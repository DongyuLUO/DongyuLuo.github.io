DROID: A Large-Scale In-The-Wild Robot Manipulation Dataset

Khazatsky, Pertsch, Nair, Balakrishna, Dasari, Karamcheti, Nasiriany, et al. · 2024 · arXiv preprint · arXiv:2403.12945 · PDF

Dongyu supplement. Added as a candidate missing/adjacent dataset paper after searching primary arXiv sources. This is a draft summary for triage, not a full paper read.

One-liner. A large open in-the-wild robot manipulation dataset: 76k trajectories, 350 hours, 564 scenes, and 84 tasks.

Setup

  • Datasets / benchmarks: Introduces DROID: 76k demonstration trajectories / 350 hours of interaction across 564 scenes and 84 tasks, collected by 50 data collectors over 12 months. The abstract states the full dataset, policy code, and hardware guide are open-sourced.
  • Hardware / simulator: Distributed real-world robot manipulation setup; the paper releases a guide for reproducing the robot hardware.

Method

Dataset paper studying whether large diverse in-the-wild data improves policy performance and generalization.

Why it matters for the map

Useful baseline for large open robot data, especially for comparing non-visual datasets against vision-heavy open manipulation corpora.

Limitations / open questions

Primarily visual/proprioceptive robot manipulation; not specifically tactile/audio/thermal language grounding.

Source note

arXiv lines 38-41 report scale, scene/task coverage, and open-source release.
