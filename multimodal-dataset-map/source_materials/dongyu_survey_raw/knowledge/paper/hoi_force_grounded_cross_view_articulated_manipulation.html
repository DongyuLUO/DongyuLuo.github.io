Hoi! - A Multimodal Dataset for Force-Grounded, Cross-View Articulated Manipulation

Engelbracht, Zurbruegg, Wohlrapp, Buechner, Valada, Pollefeys, Blum, Bauer · 2025 · arXiv preprint · arXiv:2512.04884 · PDF

Dongyu supplement. Added as a candidate missing/adjacent dataset paper after searching primary arXiv sources. This is a draft summary for triage, not a full paper read.

One-liner. A force-grounded articulated manipulation dataset spanning human and tool embodiments, designed to connect video, action, force, and tactile interaction signals.

Setup

  • Datasets / benchmarks: Introduces Hoi!, a dataset with 3,048 sequences across 381 articulated objects and 38 environments. Objects are operated in four embodiments: human hand, wrist-camera hand, handheld UMI gripper, and a custom Hoi! gripper with end-effector force and tactile sensing.
  • Hardware / simulator: Human hands, wrist-mounted camera, handheld UMI gripper, and custom Hoi! gripper. Tool embodiments provide synchronized force and tactile sensing.

Method

Dataset paper for cross-view transfer and interaction understanding from visual, action, force, and tactile streams.

Why it matters for the map

It targets exactly the under-covered force/tactile side of manipulation datasets and is useful for asking whether non-visual signals can be inferred or grounded from visual interaction.

Limitations / open questions

Language annotation is not the central signal in the abstract; it is stronger for force-grounded perception than language grounding.
