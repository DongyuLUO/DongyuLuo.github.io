Why this matters for Weiyu's line (directly on-theme): BLADE learns predicate classifiers and contact-primitive controllers from vision + language. Many of the most important manipulation predicates are not visually evaluable — is_grasped, is_inserted, is_full, surface_is_rough, is_screwed_tight live in touch, force, and sound. The threads closest to BLADE are Cluster A (touch–language grounding) — learning language-aligned predicates over tactile signals — and Cluster C (multisensory VLA policies) — contact-primitive controllers that close the loop on force/tactile/audio. Cluster F (force/proprioception policies) is the low-level control substrate; Clusters B/I/J are the representation, data, and classical-control foundations; Clusters E/H are method anchors (binding models) that are off-theme but worth knowing. Genuine open niche flagged below: language + thermal sensing is essentially empty.

A. Touch–language grounding & tactile-language models
